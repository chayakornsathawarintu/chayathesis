// 03 — Thesis & Concept

Architecture as
negotiated ground

The project investigates the liminal space between structure and void — where the drawn line dissolves into inhabitable thickness. Informed by the geological and social strata of its site, the building becomes a field of controlled tensions.

Each threshold is designed as a moment of pause, where light, section, and material converge to produce an architecture that resists immediate legibility — one that unfolds over time and through use.

// 04 — Process
01
Site Analysis

Mapping of geological, social, and infrastructural layers to extract latent organizational logics from the site's existing conditions.

02
Formal Derivation

Parametric studies of section and plan, testing the relationship between structural necessity and spatial ambiguity across iterations.

03
Material Logic

Selection of materials as indexical markers — surfaces that bear the trace of making and register time through texture and weathering.

04
Resolution

Synthesis of structural, programmatic, and atmospheric intentions into a coherent architectural proposal legible at multiple scales.
